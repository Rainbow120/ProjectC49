Chameleon

  • Chameleons are a family of lizards. Most can change the colour of their skins for camouflage, or to signal mood to other chameleons.
  • Chameleons have long sticky tongues that they can fire out in a fraction of a second. They stretch up to twice their body size. Their eyes can move independently.
  • In Disney's movie Tangled (2010), the main character, Rapunzel, has a green chameleon named Pascal!

Cicada

  • Cicadas are small insects, a lot of times confused with locusts, even though they're not related in any way.
  • Adult cicadas don't usually bite. They are usually green or black.
  • Cicadas live in temperate to tropical climates where their large size and unique buzzing sound makes them well known.

Panda

  • The giant panda is a bear, originating in south central China.
  • Although it is a carnivore, the panda's diet is 99% bamboo. Pandas in the wild occasionally eat other grasses, or even meat in the form of birds, rodents or other small animals. In captivity, they may get honey, eggs, fish, yams, shrub leaves, or citrus fruits, etc.
  • Giant panda cubs may stay with their mothers for up to three years. Then they leave her for a life of their own.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are distributed everywhere on Earth, except for the driest areas of the Sahara Desert, Antarctica, its surrounding islands, and the high Arctic.
  • Unlike other types of birds, pigeons produce a type of highly nutritious milk, that is produced by both genders to feed to their young.
  • Several species of pigeons and doves are used as food, and probably any might be. The powerful breast muscles of the family make excellent meat.

Teddy

  • Probabaly a myth, the teddy bear is said to be named after Theodore Roosevelt, a hunter and a President of the United States, whose friends gave him the nickname "Teddy", even though he didn't like being called so.
  • In Russia, bears were used as children's toys for many years before they became popular in the United States. The teddy bear in Russia has been the subject of folklore or stories for many years.
